CONCEPT

What Is Graph Intelligence?

Seven forms of insight the graph computes about itself

A dumb graph is a bag of nodes and edges you query with exact-match. A smart graph COMPUTES things about itself. Our skill graph has 155 nodes, 112 edges, and now runs 7 kinds of self-analysis on every rebuild. Each form of intelligence is a sidecar that reads the owner stack and writes a report to `~/.cache/lev/harness/skill-graph/`. None of them can write to the owner graph (per SIDECAR_POLICY.md), but they surface insights that shape human decisions.

**1. Topological validation** — TopoNetX computes Euler characteristic. If χ ≠ 4, a lane lost closure.

**2. Community detection** — NetworkX Louvain/Label Prop/Greedy agree 95.10% on the 4-lane structure. No new clusters to discover.

**3. Centrality analysis** — Betweenness centrality ranks nodes by how many paths pass through them. The bridges.

**4. Duplicate detection** — difflib fuzzy matching surfaces merge candidates within the same lane.

**5. Drift tracking** — `graph_diff.py` compares two snapshots and reports added/removed/changed.

**6. Classification prediction** — sklearn TF-IDF + LogReg suggests lanes for unlabeled skills.

**7. Scope traversal** — `nx.ancestors()` walks the `parent_id` chain: skill → lane → workstream → project.
