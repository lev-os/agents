CLOSING

On the argument

The factual record does not support 'he did all the work.' It supports: he did substantive original work on the PIC half and AIM grounding; you did substantive original work on the ATC half, FMM authority, FSM formalization, and scoring infrastructure. The turn-FSM concept and first implementation is yours by 27 days, and the canonical spec is yours. His file landed fully-formed in a single 'production ready 200/200' commit with no design trail — consistent with absorbing an idea from conversation rather than deriving it independently. The two systems are complementary, not substitutable. Combined, they dominate either alone: his breadth + your discipline, his PIC stack + your ATC stack, his AIM corpus + your verdict schema, his CallsignNormalizer + your FMM authority, his FAA citations + your honest scoring. The honest answer is not 'who won the argument' — it is 'what does each side have that the other one doesn't.' Both sides have real things. The conflict only happens when either side claims to have it all.
