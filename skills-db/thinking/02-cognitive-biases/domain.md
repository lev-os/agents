### Domain 2: **Cognitive Biases & Behavioral Economics** (120 frameworks)

**Rationale:** Largest single category (323 frameworks identified). Human decision-making systematically deviates from rationality in predictable ways. Understanding these patterns is fundamental to better thinking.

**Core content organized by Buster Benson's four problems:**

**Problem 1: Too Much Information (57 biases)**
- Confirmation bias, availability heuristic, anchoring, framing effects
- Attentional bias, mere exposure effect, context effects
- Von Restorff effect, negativity bias, picture superiority

**Problem 2: Not Enough Meaning (71 biases)**
- Clustering illusion, gamblers' fallacy, apophenia, pareidolia
- Stereotyping, halo effect, in-group bias, fundamental attribution error
- Base rate fallacy, survivorship bias, hindsight bias

**Problem 3: Need to Act Fast (56 biases)**
- Overconfidence effect, Dunning-Kruger, optimism bias
- Hyperbolic discounting, sunk cost fallacy, loss aversion, endowment effect
- Status quo bias, ambiguity effect

**Problem 4: What to Remember (32 biases)**
- Peak-end rule, misinformation effect, source confusion
- Duration neglect, serial position effects

**Behavioral Economics Frameworks:**
- Kahneman's System 1/System 2 thinking
- Prospect theory and reference dependence
- Thaler's nudge theory and choice architecture
- Ariely's predictable irrationality patterns
- Mental accounting and framing effects

**Sources:** Daniel Kahneman, Richard Thaler, Dan Ariely, Robert Cialdini, cognitive bias catalogs (200+ comprehensive enumeration)

**Skills examples:** "Recognize confirmation bias in research," "Apply nudge principles to choice design," "Use System 1/System 2 framework for decision types," "Counter anchoring in negotiations"

**Unique value:** Largest, most comprehensive cognitive bias catalog available; practical behavioral economics applications
