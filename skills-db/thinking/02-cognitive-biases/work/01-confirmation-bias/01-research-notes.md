# Research Notes: Confirmation Bias

## Origin
- **Author**: Multiple researchers, popularized by Daniel Kahneman & Amos Tversky
- **Year**: 1970s systematic study, phenomenon recognized earlier
- **Source**: "Thinking Fast and Slow" (Kahneman), Nobel Prize research

## Core Concept
Confirmation bias is the tendency to selectively filter information, search for evidence, and interpret data in ways that support pre-existing beliefs or hypotheses. Even when faced with disconfirming evidence, people unconsciously dismiss or rationalize it away. Kahneman himself admits falling prey to this bias despite decades of study, highlighting its deeply rooted nature in human cognition.
