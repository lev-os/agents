# Research Notes: Availability Heuristic

## Origin
- **Author**: Amos Tversky & Daniel Kahneman
- **Year**: 1973 (original paper "Availability: A Heuristic for Judging Frequency and Probability")
- **Source**: Published in Cognitive Psychology, later Science (1974)

## Core Concept
The availability heuristic is a mental shortcut where people evaluate the frequency or probability of events based on how easily relevant examples come to mind, rather than actual statistical frequency. Because vivid, recent, or emotionally charged examples are more mentally available, this heuristic leads to systematic bias—overestimating rare but memorable events (plane crashes) while underestimating common but mundane ones (car accidents).
