# Research Notes: Map vs. Territory

## Origin
- **Author**: Alfred Korzybski
- **Year**: 1931 (paper), 1933 (Science and Sanity book)
- **Source**: "A Non-Aristotelian System and Its Necessity for Rigour in Mathematics and Physics"

## Core Concept
"The map is not the territory" - our mental models, theories, and representations of reality are fundamentally different from reality itself. We cannot experience the world directly, only through abstractions filtered by our nervous systems and languages. The danger is confusing the model (map) with the actual reality (territory), leading to decisions based on incomplete or distorted representations.

Full quote: "A map is not the territory it represents, but, if correct, it has a similar structure to the territory, which accounts for its usefulness."

## Key Insights for SKILL.md
- Humans access reality through abstractions (nervous system + language)
- Two key limitations: biological (nervous system structure) + linguistic (language structure)
- Models are useful when they have "similar structure" to reality
- Danger of confusing representation with reality (treating models as truth)
- Application: question your assumptions, test models against reality, update maps when territory changes
