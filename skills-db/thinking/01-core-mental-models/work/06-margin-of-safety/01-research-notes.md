# Research Notes: Margin of Safety

## Origin
- **Author**: Benjamin Graham
- **Year**: 1949 (The Intelligent Investor)
- **Source**: Chapter 20 "The Margin of Safety as a Central Concept of Investment"

## Core Concept
Margin of Safety is Graham's central investment principle: only invest when the price is significantly below intrinsic value, creating a buffer against errors in valuation, unforeseen events, and market volatility. Graham said if he had to "distill the secret of sound investment into three words," it would be "Margin of Safety." The concept extends beyond investing to any decision with uncertainty - build in buffers against being wrong.
