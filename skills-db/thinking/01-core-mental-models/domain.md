## I. PROPOSED DOMAIN STRUCTURE (8 Domains)

### Domain 1: **Core Mental Models & Multidisciplinary Thinking** (80 frameworks)

**Rationale:** Foundation layer covering universal thinking tools that transcend disciplines. These are the "greatest hits"—models appearing across 3+ authoritative sources, indicating fundamental importance.

**Core content:**
- **Munger's Latticework Approach** - Interconnected multidisciplinary thinking
- **Universal models:** Inversion, first principles, second-order thinking, circle of competence, margin of safety
- **Map vs. Territory** - Models as simplifications of reality
- **Occam's & Hanlon's Razors** - Simplicity and charitable interpretation principles
- **Opportunity cost** - Hidden costs of every choice
- **Compounding effects** - Exponential growth over time
- **Leverage points** - High-impact intervention identification
- **Via negativa** - Knowledge through subtraction
- **Antifragility** - Systems benefiting from stress

**Sources:** Charlie Munger, Shane Parrish (Great Mental Models Vol 1), Farnam Street, Peter Bevelin, Gabriel Weinberg

**Skills examples:** "Apply circle of competence to business decisions," "Use inversion to identify failure modes," "Recognize opportunity costs in resource allocation," "Build latticework thinking across disciplines"

**Unique value:** Entry point for mental models thinking; teaches meta-framework for combining models
