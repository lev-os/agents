### Domain 3: **Systems Thinking & Complexity** (120 frameworks)

**Rationale:** Complex systems behave counterintuitively. Linear thinking fails in interconnected domains. This domain teaches seeing whole systems, feedback loops, and leverage points.

**Core content:**

**Donella Meadows' Systems Frameworks:**
- **12 Leverage Points** - Ranked intervention points (paradigms > goals > rules > information flows > feedback loops > parameters)
- **12 System Archetypes** - Limits to growth, tragedy of commons, shifting burden, success to successful, fixes that fail, escalation
- **Feedback Loops** - Reinforcing (exponential) vs. balancing (equilibrium-seeking)
- **Stocks, flows, delays** - System structure fundamentals
- **System traps** - Recurring failure patterns

**Peter Senge's Learning Organization:**
- Five disciplines: Personal mastery, mental models, shared vision, team learning, systems thinking
- Learning disabilities and how to overcome them
- Deep learning cycle

**Theory of Constraints (Goldratt):**
- Five focusing steps for bottleneck management
- Thinking processes: Current Reality Tree, Evaporating Cloud, Future Reality Tree
- Throughput accounting vs. cost accounting

**Complex Adaptive Systems:**
- Emergence, self-organization, nonlinearity, path dependence
- Requisite variety (Ashby's Law)
- Edge of chaos, fitness landscapes, attractors
- Agent-based dynamics and distributed intelligence

**Network Effects (16 types):**
- Direct: Physical networks, protocol networks, personal utility, personal networks
- Indirect: Marketplaces, platforms, asymptotic marketplaces
- Data network effects
- Social: Language, belief, bandwagon effects

**Santa Fe Institute Frameworks:**
- Power laws and scale-free networks
- Scaling laws (Geoffrey West)
- Small-world networks
- Computational mechanics and autocatalytic sets

**Sources:** Donella Meadows, Peter Senge, John Sterman, Eliyahu Goldratt, Santa Fe Institute, Geoffrey West, Melanie Mitchell

**Skills examples:** "Map feedback loops in organizations," "Identify leverage points for system change," "Apply system archetypes to diagnose problems," "Design for emergence and self-organization," "Understand network effects in platform businesses"

**Unique value:** Most comprehensive systems thinking toolkit; bridges theory and practice; includes cutting-edge complexity science
