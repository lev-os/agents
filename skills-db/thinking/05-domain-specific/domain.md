
### Domain 5: **Domain-Specific Models: Economics, Physics, Biology** (100 frameworks)

**Rationale:** Each scientific discipline has developed powerful mental models that transfer beautifully to everyday thinking. This domain makes specialized knowledge accessible and practical.

**Economic Models (32):**
- Supply/demand, scarcity, incentives, comparative advantage
- Economies of scale, marginal utility, elasticity, switching costs
- Sunk costs, creative destruction, monopoly dynamics
- Arbitrage, network effects, moral hazard, principal-agent problem
- Tragedy of commons, externalities, Pareto principle
- Compound interest, time value of money, diversification

**Physics & Mathematics Models (32):**
- Velocity (speed + direction), inertia, momentum, leverage, friction
- Activation energy, critical mass, relativity, half-life
- Thermodynamics (entropy, conservation), equilibrium
- Exponential vs. linear growth, compound effects, power laws
- Normal distribution vs. fat tails, regression to mean
- Probability, base rates, expected value, orders of magnitude
- Vectors, resonance, phase transitions, chaos theory

**Biological Models (22):**
- Natural selection, evolution, adaptation, survival of fittest
- Red Queen effect (running to stay in place)
- Ecosystems, niches, symbiosis, parasitism, keystone species
- Biodiversity, carrying capacity, replication
- Self-preservation, homeostasis, cooperation
- Hormesis (beneficial stress)

**Chemistry Models (12):**
- Catalysts (accelerate without being consumed)
- Activation energy, phase transitions, equilibrium
- Alloying (combinations > sum of parts)
- Half-life decay, solubility, concentration
- Chain reactions, limiting reagents

**Engineering Models (20):**
- Margin of safety, redundancy, stress testing
- Fail-safes, modularity, tight vs. loose coupling
- Feedback control, bottlenecks, emergence
- Iteration, optimization, scalability, tolerance

**Sources:** Shane Parrish (Great Mental Models Vol 2), scientific literature, cross-disciplinary applications

**Skills examples:** "Apply activation energy concept to change management," "Use evolutionary thinking for business strategy," "Recognize network effects in product design," "Apply margin of safety to project planning," "Use catalysts to accelerate organizational change"

**Unique value:** Makes scientific thinking accessible; shows how principles transfer across domains; builds intuition for complex systems
