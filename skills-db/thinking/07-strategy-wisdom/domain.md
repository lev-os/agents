
### Domain 7: **Military Strategy, Ancient Wisdom & Hidden Gems** (65 frameworks)

**Rationale:** This domain captures powerful but obscure frameworks from unexpected sources—military strategy, ancient philosophy, rationality community, and niche disciplines.

**Military Strategy Models:**
- **OODA Loop (Boyd Cycle)** - Observe, Orient, Decide, Act; tempo advantages
- Fog of war, force multipliers, center of gravity
- Maneuver warfare, Schwerpunkt (point of main effort)
- Lines of operation vs. effort

**Stoic Philosophy:**
- **Dichotomy of Control** - Focus only on what you control
- Negative visualization (premeditatio malorum)
- Memento mori (remember death), View from Above
- Voluntary discomfort, goal internalization

**Buddhist Concepts:**
- Five Aggregates (Skandhas), Dependent Origination
- Three Marks of Existence (suffering, impermanence, non-self)
- Four Noble Truths, Noble Eightfold Path
- Mindfulness, emptiness (śūnyatā)

**Taoist Principles:**
- Wu Wei (effortless action), Yin-Yang balance
- Te (virtue/power), P'u (uncarved block)
- Dialectical monism

**Rationality Community (LessWrong):**
- Epistemic vs. instrumental rationality
- Bayesian updating, Aumann's Agreement Theorem
- Schelling Fence, hyperbolic discounting
- Scope insensitivity, affect heuristic, absurdity heuristic

**Philosophical Razors:**
- Occam's, Hanlon's, Hitchens's, Hume's Guillotine
- Alder's Razor, Grice's Razor (Principle of Charity)
- Popper's falsifiability, Sagan Standard

**Hidden Gems:**
- **Lindy Effect** - Future life expectancy proportional to current age
- **Antifragility** - Gaining from disorder
- **Ergodicity** - When time average ≠ ensemble average
- Via negativa (knowledge through subtraction)
- Goodhart's Law, Chesterton's Fence
- Pre-mortem analysis, Red Team/Blue Team

**Sources:** Col. John Boyd, Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, Buddhist texts, LessWrong, Nassim Taleb, philosophical literature
