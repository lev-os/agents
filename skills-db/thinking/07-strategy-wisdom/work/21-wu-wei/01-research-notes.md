# Research Notes: Wu Wei

## Origin
- **Author**: Laozi (Lao Tzu)
- **Year**: ~6th-4th century BCE (Spring and Autumn period)
- **Source**: Tao Te Ching, Taoist philosophy

## Core Concept
Wu Wei translates as "non-action," "effortless action," or "actionlessness"—acting in harmony with the natural flow of life (the Tao) rather than forcing or resisting. It's not passivity but alignment: doing what needs to be done without unnecessary strain, interference, or ego-driven manipulation. Like water flowing around obstacles, Wu Wei achieves through yielding and adapting rather than brute force.
