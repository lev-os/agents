# Research Notes: Emptiness (Śūnyatā)

## Origin
- **Author**: Nagarjuna (Indian Buddhist philosopher)
- **Year**: ~150-250 CE
- **Source**: Madhyamaka philosophy, Buddhist texts

## Core Concept
Śūnyatā (emptiness) is the Buddhist insight that nothing possesses inherent, independent, permanent existence (svabhāva). Everything arises through dependent origination—interconnected causes and conditions. Emptiness doesn't mean "nothingness" but rather the absence of fixed essence. Understanding this loosens our grip on things we falsely believe to be solid and permanent (wealth, relationships, identity), reducing suffering caused by clinging and allowing greater freedom and compassion.
