### Domain 6: **Problem-Solving Frameworks & Creative Thinking** (70 frameworks)

**Rationale:** Distinct from decision-making (Domain 4), this focuses on structured approaches to solving novel problems and generating creative solutions.

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- 5 Whys, Fishbone diagram (Ishikawa)
- Fault Tree Analysis, FMEA (Failure Mode Effects Analysis)
- Causal Tree Analysis, change analysis

**Creative Thinking - TRIZ (40 Principles):**
- Russian innovation framework based on 200,000 patents
- 40 inventive principles: Segmentation, taking out, asymmetry, nested doll, inversion, beforehand cushioning, intermediary, self-service, blessing in disguise, etc.
- Contradiction matrix for systematic innovation

**Lateral Thinking (Edward de Bono):**
- Random entry, provocation (PO operator)
- Challenge/escape, reversal method, fractionation
- Six Thinking Hats (white, red, black, yellow, green, blue)

**Analogical Reasoning:**
- Finding parallel solutions from different domains
- Schema development for pattern recognition
- Gick & Holyoak's mapping process

**Constraint Management:**
- Theory of Constraints thinking processes
- Work breakdown structure
- Value stream mapping, constraint relaxation

**Sources:** Genrich Altshuller (TRIZ), Edward de Bono, Toyota Production System, Kepner-Tregoe, Gary Klein

**Skills examples:** "Apply TRIZ principles to product design challenges," "Use lateral thinking for breakthrough innovation," "Conduct systematic root cause analysis," "Apply analogical reasoning across domains," "Map constraints to identify bottlenecks"

**Unique value:** Only comprehensive source for TRIZ in mental models context; bridges analytical and creative problem-solving; includes lesser-known but powerful frameworks
