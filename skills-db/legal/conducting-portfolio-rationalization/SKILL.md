# Conducting Portfolio Rationalization

Structures portfolio review with strategic fit assessment, divestiture candidate identification, and proceeds redeployment analysis.

## When To Use

- Corporate leadership requests a review of business unit portfolio for strategic alignment
- Board or activist shareholders push for value-unlocking through divestitures or spin-offs
- Post-acquisition integration reveals overlapping or underperforming assets
- Capital reallocation is needed to fund growth initiatives or reduce leverage
- Market conditions shift, making certain segments structurally unattractive
- Preparing for a strategic plan cycle that requires portfolio-level prioritization

## Inputs To Gather

- **Business unit financials**: Revenue, EBITDA, margins, ROIC, and capital expenditure for each segment (trailing 3-5 years plus forecast)
- **Strategic plan and corporate thesis**: Stated growth priorities, core competency definitions, and long-range objectives
- **Market and competitive data**: Segment market size, growth rates, share trends, and competitive positioning for each business unit
- **Synergy and interdependency map**: Revenue synergies, shared services dependencies, supply chain linkages, and IP/technology overlaps across units
- **Balance sheet context**: Net debt, covenant headroom, credit rating considerations, and liquidity position
- **Tax and structural constraints**: Holding structure, tax basis in assets, trapped cash considerations, and jurisdictional restrictions on dispositions [VERIFY]
- **Prior transaction benchmarks**: Comparable divestiture multiples and recent precedent transactions in relevant sectors

## Workflow

1. **Define evaluation framework**
   - Establish strategic fit criteria: alignment with core thesis, market attractiveness, competitive position, and right-to-win assessment
   - Set financial performance thresholds: minimum ROIC vs. WACC spread, margin floors, and growth rate benchmarks
   - Weight qualitative and quantitative dimensions (e.g., 40% strategic fit, 30% financial performance, 30% market attractiveness)

2. **Score and classify each business unit**
   - Plot units on a strategic fit vs. financial performance matrix (e.g., McKinsey/GE nine-box or BCG growth-share framework adapted to context)
   - Classify into categories: **Core/Grow**, **Manage for Value**, **Fix/Turnaround**, or **Divestiture Candidate**
   - Flag units where classification is borderline — these require deeper analysis before final recommendation

3. **Assess divestiture candidates in detail**
   - Estimate standalone value: apply segment-appropriate trading and transaction multiples to unit-level EBITDA
   - Quantify separation costs: dis-synergies, stranded overhead, transition services requirements, and one-time restructuring charges
   - Identify net proceeds: gross valuation minus separation costs, tax leakage [VERIFY], and transaction fees
   - Evaluate execution risk: buyer universe depth, regulatory approval complexity, and carve-out operational readiness

4. **Model proceeds redeployment scenarios**
   - Scenario A — **Debt reduction**: Model impact on leverage ratios, interest expense savings, and credit rating implications
   - Scenario B — **Reinvestment in core**: Estimate incremental ROIC from deploying proceeds into Core/Grow segments via organic investment or bolt-on M&A
   - Scenario C — **Shareholder return**: Quantify per-share impact of buybacks or special dividends at current valuation
   - Compare scenarios on a risk-adjusted net present value basis to determine optimal redeployment mix

5. **Develop sequencing and execution roadmap**
   - Prioritize divestitures by execution readiness, market timing, and interdependency constraints (e.g., divest Unit A before Unit B if shared services must be unwound sequentially)
   - Identify pre-sale value enhancement actions: management carve-out, cost rationalization, or contract renegotiation to improve standalone attractiveness
   - Establish timeline milestones: board approval, advisor engagement, data room preparation, buyer outreach, and targeted closing windows

## Output

- **Portfolio scorecard matrix**: Visual summary of all business units scored across strategic fit and financial performance dimensions with clear category assignments
- **Divestiture candidate profiles**: For each recommended disposition — standalone valuation range, separation cost estimate, net proceeds, buyer universe summary, and key execution risks
- **Proceeds redeployment analysis**: Side-by-side comparison of deployment scenarios with NPV, leverage impact, and EPS/ROIC accretion/dilution metrics
- **Execution roadmap**: Sequenced timeline with milestones, dependencies, and pre-sale workstreams
- **Board-ready recommendation memo**: Synthesized narrative linking portfolio actions to shareholder value creation thesis with quantified impact

## Quality Checks

- Confirm ROIC calculations use consistent capital base definitions across all business units — inconsistent methodology distorts relative scoring
- Validate that divestiture valuation multiples reflect segment-specific comparables, not blended corporate multiples
- Verify separation cost estimates include dis-synergies and stranded costs, not just direct transaction expenses
- Ensure tax leakage estimates have been reviewed against actual holding structures and asset tax basis [VERIFY]
- Cross-check that proceeds redeployment scenarios use realistic reinvestment return assumptions, not aspirational plan figures
- Confirm interdependency analysis accounts for shared customer relationships, not just shared cost centers
- Flag any unit classification that changed materially from prior rationalization reviews — explain the driver of the shift
