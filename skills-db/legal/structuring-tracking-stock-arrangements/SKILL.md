# Structuring Tracking Stock Arrangements

Designs tracking stock structures with attributed business economics, intergroup arrangements, and value realization mechanics for multi-business enterprises seeking to unlock value without full separation.

## When To Use

- Parent company wants market-visible value attribution for a high-growth division without surrendering control or tax-free reorganization eligibility
- Board is evaluating partial separation alternatives (tracking stock vs. spin-off vs. carve-out IPO vs. equity carve-out) and needs a structured comparison
- Existing tracking stock arrangement requires restructuring due to changed business economics, intergroup friction, or conversion/redemption triggers
- Shareholder activism or analyst pressure demands transparent segment-level economics for a conglomerate discount situation

## Inputs To Gather

- **Corporate structure**: Parent entity, attributed business units, and retained businesses with current org chart and legal entity mapping
- **Financial data**: Segment-level P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow for each attributed business (minimum 3 years historical plus projections)
- **Intergroup economics**: Existing or proposed cost allocations, shared services pricing, intercompany lending terms, and tax sharing arrangements
- **Capital structure details**: Outstanding share classes, debt covenants referencing consolidated vs. segment metrics, and any existing preferred/convertible instruments
- **Strategic objectives**: Target outcomes (value transparency, employee equity incentives, acquisition currency, eventual full separation pathway)
- **Governance framework**: Board composition rules, voting rights allocation (one vote vs. differential), and fiduciary duty structure across stockholder groups
- **Tax and regulatory context**: Jurisdiction of incorporation, IRS ruling history on tracking stock treatment, SEC reporting requirements [VERIFY: current SEC staff position on tracking stock registration and reporting]

## Workflow

1. **Define the attributed business perimeter**
   - Identify which assets, liabilities, revenues, and costs attribute to the tracked group vs. the retained group
   - Map shared assets and determine attribution methodology (direct allocation, usage-based, revenue-proportional)
   - Flag assets or contracts that cannot be cleanly attributed and propose treatment

2. **Design intergroup economic arrangements**
   - Set intergroup interest rates on attributed net debt/net cash positions
   - Define cost-sharing formulas for corporate overhead, shared infrastructure, and corporate-level functions (tax, treasury, legal)
   - Establish transfer pricing methodology for intercompany goods/services with arm's-length benchmarking
   - Draft intergroup tax sharing agreement allocating consolidated tax liability [VERIFY: state-level tax sharing rules vary by jurisdiction]

3. **Structure the equity and governance mechanics**
   - Determine tracking stock share class (common vs. preferred attributes), voting rights, and dividend policy linkage to attributed earnings
   - Define the ratio policy — how tracked group shares relate economically to the attributed business (e.g., 1:1, fraction-of-interest)
   - Specify board representation and committee structure for oversight of intergroup fairness
   - Draft fiduciary duty framework: directors owe duties to all common stockholders, but policy statements can guide attributed-interest balancing [VERIFY: Delaware case law on tracking stock fiduciary duties — interpret under current Chancery guidance]

4. **Build value realization and exit mechanics**
   - Design conversion rights: mandatory vs. optional conversion of tracking stock into parent common or spun-off entity equity
   - Set redemption triggers (time-based, performance-based, change-of-control) with pricing methodology (VWAP, appraisal, formula-based)
   - Map the full separation pathway — conditions under which tracking stock converts into an independent public company via spin-off or split-off
   - Define anti-dilution protections and adjustments for intergroup transactions (asset transfers, capital contributions, dividend recapitalizations)

5. **Assess risks and structural vulnerabilities**
   - Evaluate conglomerate discount persistence risk — tracking stocks historically trade at discounts due to governance complexity and lack of true separation
   - Analyze intergroup conflict scenarios (competing capital needs, cross-subsidization disputes, divergent strategic priorities)
   - Stress-test the structure under downside cases: attributed business underperformance, parent liquidity crisis, hostile acquisition of parent
   - Review litigation risk from minority tracking stockholders alleging intergroup self-dealing

## Output

Deliver a **Tracking Stock Structure Report** containing:

- **Executive summary**: Recommended structure with rationale and key trade-offs vs. alternative separation forms
- **Attributed business definition**: Precise scope of assets, liabilities, revenues, and costs with attribution methodology
- **Intergroup arrangements**: Cost allocation, tax sharing, transfer pricing, and intercompany financing terms in tabular format
- **Equity and governance design**: Share class terms, voting rights, dividend policy, board representation, and fiduciary framework
- **Value realization mechanics**: Conversion, redemption, and full-separation pathway with trigger conditions and pricing formulas
- **Risk matrix**: Identified structural vulnerabilities ranked by likelihood and impact, with proposed mitigants
- **Comparison table**: Side-by-side evaluation of tracking stock vs. spin-off vs. carve-out IPO on key dimensions (control retention, tax efficiency, value transparency, execution complexity, reversibility)

## Quality Checks

- Verify that attributed business financials reconcile to consolidated segment reporting with no unallocated gaps
- Confirm intergroup pricing passes arm's-length standards and does not create hidden value transfers between stockholder groups
- Ensure conversion and redemption mechanics are internally consistent (no circular references, no scenarios where both trigger simultaneously without resolution)
- Validate that governance structure addresses fiduciary duty conflicts — a single board overseeing competing stockholder interests requires explicit fairness review protocols
- Check that debt covenant compliance is maintained at both the consolidated and attributed-business level under the proposed structure
- Confirm all [VERIFY] items are resolved against current statutory and regulatory authority before finalizing
