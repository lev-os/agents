## 1. Defending Depositions

When defending, the deposition package is fundamentally a risk-management tool. Produce:

### Anticipated Topics and Exhibits
Predict the adversary's likely examination path based on pleadings and produced documents. Map each anticipated topic to your vulnerabilities and strengths.

### Vulnerabilities and Safe Themes
Help the witness stay within personal knowledge and avoid speculation. Identify areas where the witness is likely to be pushed into privileged territory or beyond their knowledge.

### Ethics-Safe Prep Session Agenda
Include:
- Review of key documents
- Review of prior statements
- Walk-through of the likely question sequence
- Practice on answering concisely

Behavioral guidance to cover:
- Listen to the full question
- Pause before answering
- Answer only the question asked
- Understand the distinction between "I don't recall" and "I don't know"
- Do not volunteer information beyond the question

The agenda must explicitly prohibit coaching false testimony.

### Objection and Privilege Framework
Tie specific reminders to FRCP 30(c)(2). In federal court, speaking objections—those that suggest an answer to the witness (e.g., "Objection, he already answered that, he's saying he doesn't know")—are prohibited. The objecting attorney may only state the legal basis concisely ("Objection, form").

Some state courts have even more restrictive provisions (e.g., New York Part 221). Verify the objection practice rules for the specific forum.

Under FRCP 30(c)(2), instruction not to answer is permitted only to:
- Preserve a privilege
- Enforce a court-ordered limitation
- Present a motion under FRCP 30(d)(3)

The outline should avoid creating a sanctionable instruction pattern.

### Rehabilitation Topics
Plan re-examination topics for areas where the witness may have been led into confusing or misleading testimony. Keep these focused—rehabilitation is about correcting the record, not retrying the case.

## 2. FRCP 30(b)(6) Corporate Representatives

The outline must be structured around the noticed topics because the "reasonable particularity" requirement for the notice shapes both scope and objections.

### Taking a 30(b)(6)

**Structure:** Organize by noticed topic, not chronologically.

**Preparation adequacy questions:** The organization must designate persons to testify about information "known or reasonably available" to the organization. Test preparation:
- "Who did you speak with to prepare?"
- "What repositories did you search?"
- "What policies did you review?"
- "Were there any topics on the notice that you feel you were unable to adequately prepare for?"

**Bind-the-corporation strategy:** Because the designee speaks for the organization, admissions are party-opponent statements. Plan commit points accordingly—these bind the entity, not just the individual.

**Scope fences:** Many courts interpret topics with reasonable flexibility, but the outline should identify where questions go beyond noticed topics so defending counsel can object when appropriate.

**Inadequate preparation:** Can support a motion to compel or additional relief depending on the court's practice. Flag the specific consequences as requiring attorney verification for the forum.

### Defending a 30(b)(6)

Request the notice topic list and identify all designated witnesses. Ensure:
- Each topic has a designated witness who has been adequately prepared
- The witness understands they speak for the organization, not personally
- Preparation covers the organization's knowledge, not just the individual's
- Scope boundaries are mapped so you can object when opposing counsel strays

## 3. Expert Witnesses

Expert depositions serve a different strategic function: locking in opinions and building the record for reliability challenges (Daubert/FRE 702 motions) or motions in limine.

### Outline Structure for Taking an Expert Deposition

Lock in four categories:
1. **Opinions stated in the report** — get the expert to confirm each opinion and state whether the report is complete
2. **Bases and data considered** — everything relied on, everything not relied on, everything considered but rejected
3. **Methodology and its application** — the specific steps taken, not just the general methodology name
4. **Concessions helpful to reliability challenges** — the high-value targets

### Concession Targets

Plan concrete concession targets for each expert:
- Assumptions not tested
- Alternative causes not ruled out
- Missing data the expert did not have or chose not to use
- Litigation-driven selection bias (was the expert hired to reach a conclusion?)
- Compensation and repeat-player patterns (how many times retained by this firm?)
- Prior exclusions by other courts
- Limits on generalization (does the opinion apply beyond this specific case?)
- Differences between the expert's methodology and standard practice in their field

### FRE 702 (2023 Amendment)

The December 1, 2023 amendment clarified that:
- The proponent must establish admissibility by a preponderance of the evidence
- Reliability requirements apply to both the opinion and its application to the facts

Track the rule text from a verifiable source. In state courts, the governing expert admissibility standard may differ (Frye vs. Daubert). Do not state which standard applies without jurisdiction confirmation.

### Defending an Expert Deposition

Focus the prep on:
- Ensuring the expert can explain methodology in plain language
- Identifying the weakest points in the opinion and preparing responses
- Reviewing the report line by line for precision
- Preparing for compensation and bias questioning
- Ensuring the expert does not overstate conclusions

## 4. Apex Witnesses

Some jurisdictions impose heightened scrutiny or require a showing of unique personal knowledge before deposing high-level executives. The apex doctrine is not uniform across jurisdictions.

### When Taking an Apex Deposition

Ask for the jurisdiction and whether a protective order motion is anticipated. Prepare to justify the deposition by demonstrating:
- The executive has unique personal knowledge not available from other sources
- Lower-level employees have already been deposed and the information gap remains
- The topics are directly relevant to claims or defenses

### When Defending an Apex Deposition

Prepare to support limitation or protective relief by showing:
- The executive lacks personal knowledge on the noticed topics
- The information is available from other corporate witnesses
- The deposition is being sought for harassment or strategic pressure

### General Apex Guidance

Treat all apex doctrine statements as jurisdiction-dependent. Request the controlling jurisdiction and any relevant protective order precedent or local practice notes. Absent that, provide only general risk framing and flag the issue for attorney verification.
