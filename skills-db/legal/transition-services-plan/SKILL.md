# Transition Services Plan

Drafts a TSA exhibit to an Asset Purchase Agreement governing post-closing seller support for operational continuity across customer, vendor, HR, finance, and IT functions.

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed or near-final APA** — Closing Date, defined terms, notice provisions, indemnification framework
2. **Functional area inventory** — departments/operations requiring transition support
3. **Key personnel lists** — designated contacts per party per functional area
4. **Duration parameters** — agreed support periods per service category
5. **Compensation structure** — included in Purchase Price or separately compensated

## Quick Start

1. Collect APA terms, functional area inventory, and personnel lists
2. Build the functional area matrix (one row per service category)
3. Draft sections in order: header → definitions → scope → standards → compensation → governance → term → APA relationship → signatures
4. Cross-check all defined terms and dates against APA
5. Validate each area has measurable completion criteria

## Document Header

TRANSITION SERVICES PLAN

Exhibit [__] to that certain Asset Purchase Agreement dated [DATE]
by and between [SELLER LEGAL NAME] ("Seller") and [BUYER LEGAL NAME] ("Buyer")

Effective Date: [Closing Date]
Expiration Date: [DATE], subject to extension per Section [__]

## Section Outline

| # | Section | Content |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | Definitions & Interpretation | Incorporate APA defined terms; define Plan-specific terms |
| 2 | Scope of Services | Functional area matrix |
| 3 | Service Standards | Consistent with Seller's historical practices; commercially reasonable efforts |
| 4 | Compensation | Included in Purchase Price OR separate fee schedule with rates/payment terms |
| 5 | Governance | Transition coordinators, meeting cadence, escalation path |
| 6 | Term & Termination | Commencement, expiration, early termination, extension mechanics |
| 7 | Relationship to APA | APA controls on conflict; confidentiality/indemnification incorporated; no partnership/employment |
| 8 | Signatures | Authorized representatives with name/title/date blocks |

## Functional Area Matrix

Draft one row per service category with these columns:

**Service Category** · **Scope of Services** · **Seller Contact** (name/title/phone/email) · **Buyer Contact** (name/title/phone/email) · **Duration** (start–end) · **Milestones/Deliverables** (measurable completion criteria)

### Standard Functional Areas

| Area | Typical Scope | Duration |
|------|---------------|----------|
| Customer/Client Transition | Introductions, relationship history, file transfer, ownership communication | 30–60 days |
| Vendor/Supplier Management | Account transfer/assignment, pricing history, qualification processes | 45–90 days |
| Employee/HR Integration | Onboarding support, personnel files, policy explanation, employee relations | 60–90 days |
| Financial/Accounting | Record transfer, book closing, banking transition, reconciliations | 60–90 days (min. one full cycle) |
| IT Systems & Data | Data migration, license transfer, credentials, training, integration | 60–90+ days |

## Compensation Clause Templates

**Included in Purchase Price:**
> The transition services described herein are included within the Purchase Price set forth in the Asset Purchase Agreement. No additional compensation shall be due to Seller except as set forth in a separate written agreement.

**Separately compensated:**
> Seller shall be compensated for transition services at the rates set forth in Schedule [__]. Buyer shall reimburse Seller for pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses within [30] days of invoice.

## Governance Framework

- Designate one Transition Coordinator per party
- Weekly or bi-weekly status meetings with written progress reports
- Escalation path: Coordinator → Senior Management → APA dispute resolution
- Modifications by written agreement only

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **APA consistency** — cross-check all defined terms, dates, and cross-references against APA
- **Realistic timelines** — include extension mechanisms; avoid rigid deadlines without cure periods
- **Measurable deliverables** — concrete completion criteria per area, not open-ended obligations
- **Seller burden** — obligations must not unreasonably interfere with Seller's retained operations
- **Confidentiality** — incorporate APA provisions by reference; add data-handling specifics for IT/HR
- **No agency/employment** — explicit disclaimer: no employment, partnership, or joint venture created
- **Expense caps** — if reimbursable, include pre-approval thresholds and caps
- **Jurisdiction** — same governing law and forum selection as APA
