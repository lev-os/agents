# Preparing Quarterly Lp Reports

Creates comprehensive quarterly LP reporting packages with fund performance, portfolio updates, and market commentary for institutional investors.

## When To Use

- Preparing end-of-quarter LP update packages (Q1–Q4 reporting cycles)
- Drafting fund-level performance letters and portfolio narratives for investor distribution
- Structuring capital account statements, fee/expense disclosures, and portfolio company updates
- Building ILPA-compliant reporting templates for new fund launches
- Responding to LP requests for interim or ad-hoc performance updates

## Inputs To Gather

- **Fund financials**: NAV, gross/net IRR, TVPI, DPI, RVPI as of quarter-end; confirm audited vs. estimated status
- **Capital activity**: Contributions called, distributions made, recallable amounts, unfunded commitments by LP
- **Portfolio company data**: Revenue, EBITDA, valuation marks (and methodology — comparable transactions, DCF, market multiples), material events (exits, add-ons, write-downs)
- **Fee and expense detail**: Management fees, fund expenses, organizational costs, carried interest accrual status
- **Market commentary inputs**: Sector trends, macroeconomic context, deal pipeline observations from investment team
- **LP-specific requirements**: Any bespoke reporting obligations from side letters (e.g., ESG disclosures, co-invest reporting, UBTI/ECI breakdowns)
- **Prior quarter report**: For consistency in format, tone, and narrative continuity

## Workflow

1. **Confirm reporting scope** — Identify the quarter-end date, fund(s) in scope, and whether reports are estimated or based on audited figures. Flag any portfolio companies with pending valuation changes.
2. **Assemble capital account data** — Pull per-LP capital account statements showing beginning balance, contributions, distributions, net income allocation, and ending balance. Reconcile to fund-level totals. [VERIFY] allocation methodology matches LPA waterfall provisions.
3. **Compile performance metrics** — Calculate gross and net IRR, TVPI, DPI, RVPI at the fund level and (if required) by vintage year or strategy sleeve. State the valuation date and note any lag in underlying data. [VERIFY] performance calculation methodology (e.g., since-inception vs. annualized) is consistent with prior periods and LPA/side-letter commitments.
4. **Draft portfolio company updates** — For each active holding, summarize operating performance (revenue, EBITDA, key KPIs), current valuation mark and basis, and material developments (management changes, refinancings, bolt-on acquisitions, exit processes). Flag unrealized vs. realized gains/losses.
5. **Prepare fee and expense disclosure** — Itemize management fees, fund-level expenses, and any offsets (e.g., monitoring fees, transaction fees credited to the fund). Disclose carried interest accrual and clawback status if applicable. [VERIFY] fee disclosures align with SEC reporting requirements and any ILPA fee transparency template commitments.
6. **Write GP letter / market commentary** — Draft a narrative letter from the GP covering fund strategy execution, market environment, portfolio highlights, and forward outlook. Keep tone factual and measured; avoid forward-looking performance projections that could create liability.
7. **Integrate ESG and supplemental reporting** — If required by side letters or fund terms, include ESG metrics, diversity data, co-investment activity summaries, or sector-specific disclosures. [VERIFY] which LPs have bespoke reporting obligations.
8. **Assemble the complete package** — Combine the GP letter, capital account statements, performance summary, portfolio company detail, fee disclosures, and supplemental sections into a unified report. Ensure consistent formatting, defined terms, and page numbering.
9. **Review and approve** — Route the draft through investment team (for portfolio accuracy), finance/accounting (for capital accounts and fees), legal/compliance (for regulatory disclosures), and IR (for tone and LP relationship context) before distribution.

## Output

A quarterly LP reporting package containing:

- **GP letter**: 1–3 page narrative covering fund activity, market context, and portfolio highlights
- **Fund performance summary**: Table of gross/net IRR, multiples (TVPI, DPI, RVPI), and benchmark comparisons if applicable
- **Capital account statements**: Per-LP breakdown of contributions, distributions, ending NAV, and unfunded commitment
- **Portfolio company updates**: Individual holding summaries with operating metrics, valuation, and material events
- **Fee and expense schedule**: Itemized management fees, expenses, offsets, and carried interest status
- **Supplemental disclosures**: ESG reporting, co-invest summaries, or other side-letter-driven addenda as applicable

## Quality Checks

- All performance figures tie to fund administrator records; any estimated figures are clearly labeled as such
- Capital account totals reconcile to fund-level NAV with no unexplained variances
- Valuation methodology for each holding is stated and consistent with prior quarters (or changes are explained)
- Fee disclosures match LPA terms and comply with ILPA fee reporting principles [VERIFY]
- No forward-looking return projections or guarantees appear in the GP letter
- Side-letter-specific reporting obligations are satisfied for each LP receiving the package [VERIFY]
- Report formatting, defined terms, and fund naming conventions are consistent throughout
- Sensitive data (individual LP names, allocation percentages) is excluded from any sections shared across the LP base
- Draft has been reviewed by investment, finance, legal/compliance, and IR teams before distribution
