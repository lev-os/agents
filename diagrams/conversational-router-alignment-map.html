Conversational Boundary Proposal

Lev already has the thesis. It is missing the boundary contract.

The 30-agent doc scan converged on one main conclusion: the repo already knows that conversation is the ephemeral proposal surface, alignment is the gate, graph patches are the state transition, and execution belongs to the existing runtime planes. What is still missing is one canonical contract for typed conversational intent, human approval semantics, execution handoff, and replayable decision memory.

Core Thesis

Conversation is not execution. It is the gated front door.

The scan consistently separates four layers: conversational ingress, typed proposal/graph-patch admission, constrained runtime execution, and human-loop surfaces. The strongest recurring mistake in the docs is when one of those layers tries to impersonate another.

1. Conversation

Ephemeral understanding surface. Good for routing, clarifying, and shaping proposals. Bad as a hidden executor.

2. Alignment Gate

Typed intent, approval, clarification, and proposal admission. This is the missing canonical contract.

3. Runtime

FlowMind -> Orchestration/Exec -> Event Bus/Graph. Existing planes stay intact.

4. Surfaces

AgentPing, CLI, dashboard, voice, and future hosts present steering/approval affordances but should not own policy or runtime truth.

Drivers

Architectural Drivers And Non-Negotiables

Drivers

  • Governed promotion from conversation into tracked state and execution.
  • Preserve plane integrity: no UI, skill, middleware, or dashboard shadow orchestrator.
  • Portable human-loop semantics across CLI, AgentPing, dashboard, and voice.
  • Evidence-first trust: replay, provenance, validation, and typed artifacts over prompt rhetoric.
  • Canon compression: absorb earlier near-solutions instead of inventing a fourth version.

Already Decided

  • Three-plane plus event-spine split remains intact.
  • .flow.yaml / GraphDocument stay canonical route/IR surfaces.
  • S14 execution-contract ownership wins over earlier drafts.
  • Synthetic judges remain advisory; humans plus evidence gates decide promotion.
  • bd remains the canonical work-tracking surface.
Most important synthesis: The philosophy is ahead of the contract. The proposal should not create more philosophy. It should create the missing typed boundary object and the approval semantics around it.

Brainstorm

Best Next Questions To Discuss

These are the highest-leverage questions that fall out of the scan. Answering even one of them would meaningfully narrow the follow-up spec or ADR work.

1. What exactly is “aligned enough”?

Is alignment a boolean approval state, a typed review outcome, a confidence threshold plus HITL override, or something richer tied to scope and risk?

2. Where should decision replay live?

Memory, Domain, Graph, or an adjacent approval-history contract? The scan says this is one of the biggest missing links.

3. How much should the word “router” matter?

The architecture supports the idea strongly, but multiple lanes suggest “alignment boundary” may be more canonical than a sweeping router-first rebrand.

4. How much co-creation belongs beyond approvals?

AgentPing already has approval, selection, input, and escalation. Do you want richer collaborative editing semantics in the first canonical contract, or keep v1 narrowly approval-centric?
