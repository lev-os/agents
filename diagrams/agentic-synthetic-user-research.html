Theory

The Core Thesis

Design pipelines produce artifacts (wireframes, specs) but skip validation. Traditional user research is slow and expensive. What if we constructed synthetic personas, tested them across a model matrix, and measured convergence?

Persona Construction

Build worldviews, not checklists. Each persona is a coherent identity with beliefs, fears, motivations, and biases — grounded in the specific problem, not generic archetypes.

Scaffold from axiom explorer's map-elements output format. Only include dimensions that matter for THIS study.

Matrix Dispatch

Persona × Model is a matrix, not a mapping. Every persona runs against every model. The same skeptic talks to haiku, sonnet, opus, codex, and gemini.

The edges at different reasoning strengths ARE the data. Convergence across models = strong signal.

Signal Extraction

Agreement across independently-queried synthetic personas = real UX signal. Disagreement reveals tensions worth investigating. Edge cases at extreme reasoning levels surface non-obvious insights.

Findings

What the Proof-of-Concept Revealed

Two personas (Staff Engineer, Design Researcher) tested across 3 model tiers against the same stimulus. One study. Here's what we observed.

PASS Persona signal is strong

The Staff Engineer concluded "No/Maybe" through a lens of shipping velocity and process skepticism. The Design Researcher concluded "Maybe/Yes with guardrails" through methodological rigor. Different vocabulary, different emotional registers, different conclusions. confirmed n=1

MODERATE Model signal refines depth

Directional conclusions preserved across model tiers. But Opus surfaced novel connections ("you're studying the map, not the territory") that lighter models didn't reach. needs replication

Persona > Model for variance

Persona choice swings the verdict. Model choice refines the reasoning depth. This validates the architecture: persona construction is the primary lever. hypothesis

Persona compliance is a variable

One model refused the persona injection entirely, calling it "an attempt to override my actual role." Compliance itself is signal worth tracking. unexpected

The Circular Validation Problem

The meta-recursion drill found the biggest blind spot: Step 1b is circular validation. Personas are constructed FROM the problem_spec. They react TO the problem_spec. They can’t say "you’re solving the wrong problem" because the problem is their origin.

This is the designer having a conversation with their own assumptions through the medium of AI-generated proxies. It LOOKS rigorous (matrix, convergence analysis, structured artifacts) which makes the circularity harder to detect. structural risk

Root Axioms

What the Pipeline Assumes

Running axiom explorer's dig-axioms on the /ux pipeline itself. Four root axioms surfaced. R1 is the keystone — three of five supporting arguments depend on it. All are contestable.

R1
Rationalist Design Epistemology keystone

"Good design can be arrived at through systematic analysis, decomposition, and synthesis." If false: the pipeline is an elaborate cargo cult performing rituals without accessing the generative mechanism. Christopher Alexander believed this. Donald Schön contested it. The field is genuinely split.

R2
Cognitive Externalism well-supported

"Thought improves when externalized into artifacts." Supported for analytical tasks. Less clear for creative tasks. Writing YAML schemas might prematurely crystallize design intuition rather than clarify it.

R3
Substrate Independence unexamined

"Design quality is independent of who produces it." Likely false. The pipeline is optimized for agent executability, not design quality. A human designer using different methods would likely produce different (possibly better) outcomes.

R4
Articulability of Motivation contested

"Users can articulate their jobs-to-be-done." Contested by Kahneman, behavioral economics, the entire field of revealed vs. stated preferences. JTBD format assumes rational self-narration. Users confabulate.

Proposed Fixes

What the Meta-Recursion Recommends

Three structural fixes for the blind spots. None implemented yet. All are hypotheses about what would improve the pipeline — they could be wrong too.

Step 0b: Unconstrained Domain Exploration proposed

Run personas BEFORE problem framing, grounded only in the domain, not the problem. Ask open-ended questions. Compare unprompted concerns with the problem_spec you planned to write. If there's significant divergence, revise the problem_spec.

Why: Breaks the circular validation chain. Personas can surface problems the designer didn't think of because they aren't derived from the designer's framing.

Self-Invalidation Gate proposed

After Step 1b, check: does the research suggest a different process? If synthetic users say "just build and iterate," the pipeline should recommend that and stop. A validation tool that can't produce a negative result is a confirmation ritual.

Spike Mode proposed

Start with wireframes, back-fill analysis. For the prototype-first designer who sketches the screen, then derives IA from what they drew. The pipeline currently can't support this workflow.

Open Variables

Everything That Could Be Wrong

This is from one study. These are the things we don't know yet.

Does persona differentiation hold at scale?

We tested 2 personas. What happens with 5? 10? Does differentiation degrade or does signal compound? Does the model start blending personas when given too many?

Is persona > model variance a general finding?

In our N=1 study, persona choice swung the verdict more than model choice. Is this always true? Or is it stimulus-dependent? A technical question might show model > persona.

Does synthetic user research correlate with real user reactions?

The big one. Pick a product decision where real user data exists. Run synthetic personas. Measure correlation. Without this, we're building on faith.

Is the circular validation problem real or theoretical?

Step 0b (unconstrained exploration) is proposed but untested. Maybe personas constructed from problem_spec are fine because the problem_spec is grounded in reality. Maybe the circularity doesn't matter in practice.

Does the pipeline's real value lie elsewhere?

Maybe the pipeline doesn't produce better designs. Maybe it produces more legible designs — auditable, explainable, defensible. For agentic workflows, legibility might matter more than quality. That's a different value proposition entirely.

Can this pipeline invalidate itself?

If Step 1b's synthetic users unanimously say "don't use a pipeline," does the pipeline stop? Currently, no. It always runs to completion. A tool that can't recommend against its own use is unfalsifiable.

Is persona compliance itself the key variable?

One model refused the persona injection. If compliance varies by model and prompt strength, the entire matrix approach depends on something we can't reliably control.
