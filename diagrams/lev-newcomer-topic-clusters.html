Reader Failure Modes

A zero-context reader will get lost fast unless the unified explainer deliberately fixes the problems below. These are content-system failures, not just copy-edit issues.

Failure 1

Naming drift

Lev, Leviathan, AgentPing, A2UI, Kingly, PromptOS, COPI, and Nanograph all appear without a stable explanation of what kind of thing each one is.

Failure 2

Competing "main" pages

The folder contains multiple top-level synthesis pages. A newcomer cannot tell which one is current, canonical, or only an intermediate step.

Failure 3

Example scarcity

The architecture is strong, but the material still lacks dense, repeated everyday examples that make the ideas feel obvious.

Failure 4

Proof without labeling

Screenshots, prototypes, Storybook states, and research outputs are mixed together. Without labels, readers overestimate maturity or misread supporting evidence as commitment.

Proposed Segment Model

The unified explainer should teach Lev through eight sections. Each section starts in normal language, then introduces the internal term, then grounds the idea in examples and shipped proof.

Segment 1

The one-minute explanation and naming discipline layer.

Build Loop

The section model only becomes real if it drives the production loop. The right flow is: write in markdown, render per section, audit in zero context, validate in browser, then merge into the single explainer.

1. Write

Lock the section contract in markdown and make every concept legible in normal language first.

2. Render

Turn each section into an HTML partial with a consistent structure and explicit evidence labels: runtime, prototype, storybook, audit, reference.

3. Audit

Use a zero-context reader to find where the section still assumes too much prior knowledge.

4. Merge

Combine only the sections that pass the comprehension and browser checks into the final `/diagrams` replacement.
